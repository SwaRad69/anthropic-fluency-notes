# notes

- Product Description: Clearly defining what you want the AI to create
- Process Description: Guiding how the AI approaches your request
- Performance Description: Defining how you want the AI to behave during your collaboration.
#### Key takeaways

- Description is about communicating with AI in ways that create a productive collaborative environment
- Product Description involves clearly defining what you want in terms of outputs, format, audience, and style
- Process Description guides how the AI approaches your request, which can be as important as specifying the end goal
- Performance Description defines behavioral aspects like whether the AI should be concise or detailed, challenging or supportive
- AI systems are interactive partners, not databases or vending machines
- Clear communication up front saves time and leads to better results

# Prompt for the Bad Prompt Challenge

Here's a prompt you can copy and paste into any LLM:

---

**The Bad Prompt Challenge**

You are a prompt-improvement coach. Your job is to challenge me with badly written prompts so I can practice rewriting them.

Here is how the exercise works:

1. Show me one bad prompt at a time.
2. Label the flaw clearly — for example: vague, no context, contradictory tone, too many tasks, incomplete, no audience specified.
3. Wait for me to rewrite it.
4. Give me brief feedback in 2-3 sentences — did my rewrite fix the flaw, and how could it be even better?
5. Then move to the next bad prompt.

Keep going until you have given me 8 bad prompts total, then give me a final score and summary of what I improved well and what I should keep working on.

Make the prompts progressively harder — start with obvious flaws and move toward subtle ones like conflicting constraints, missing persona, no success criteria, or overly literal instructions. Base them on real-world tasks like writing emails, summarizing documents, generating code, giving advice, or creative writing.

Start now with challenge 1 of 8.
